# Mystery Novel Structure Template

## Genre Characteristics
- Central puzzle/crime to solve
- Clues planted fairly
- Red herrings
- Satisfying reveal
- Detective/protagonist solving
- Timeline precision สำคัญมาก

## Recommended Length
- **Chapters:** 20-30 บท
- **Words/Chapter:** 3,500-5,000 คำ
- **Total:** 70,000-150,000 คำ

---

## ACT 1: THE CRIME (บทที่ 1-6)

### Chapter 1-2: The Setup
**Goals:**
- Establish setting และบรรยากาศ
- แนะนำ detective/protagonist
- Introduce key characters (suspects)
- Normal life before crime

**Mystery Elements:**
- Plant subtle clues
- Establish relationships
- Show potential motives

### Chapter 3-4: The Crime
**Goals:**
- Crime discovered (murder/theft/disappearance)
- Initial shock and reaction
- Detective gets involved
- Crime scene details

**Important:**
- Describe crime scene carefully
- Note what's present AND missing
- First impressions (often wrong)

### Chapter 5-6: Initial Investigation
**Goals:**
- Examine evidence
- Interview first witnesses
- Establish timeline
- List suspects

---

## ACT 2A: INVESTIGATION (บทที่ 7-14)

### Chapter 7-9: Gathering Clues
**Goals:**
- Interview suspects
- Discover alibis
- Find physical evidence
- Uncover secrets

**Clue Distribution:**
- Major clue every 2-3 chapters
- Minor clues throughout
- Red herrings mixed in
- Track what detective knows

### Chapter 10-12: Complications
**Goals:**
- Alibis don't hold up
- New suspects emerge
- Second crime (optional)
- Detective faces obstacles

### Chapter 13-14: Midpoint
**Goals:**
- Major revelation
- Case seems to change
- Detective re-examines assumptions
- New direction

---

## ACT 2B: DEEPER (บทที่ 15-22)

### Chapter 15-17: Second Investigation
**Goals:**
- New avenue of inquiry
- Deeper into suspects' lives
- Connections emerge
- More secrets revealed

### Chapter 18-20: False Solution
**Goals:**
- Detective thinks they solved it
- But something doesn't fit
- Or another crime happens
- Back to square one

### Chapter 21-22: The Key
**Goals:**
- Find crucial missing piece
- Connection becomes clear
- Detective knows who but needs proof
- Setup for climax

---

## ACT 3: THE SOLUTION (บทที่ 23-28)

### Chapter 23-24: Gathering Proof
**Goals:**
- Set trap for culprit
- Get necessary evidence
- Confront witnesses
- Build case

### Chapter 25-26: The Confrontation
**Goals:**
- Face the culprit
- Danger for detective (optional)
- The reveal begins

### Chapter 27: The Reveal
**Goals:**
- Explain how and why
- Show how clues fit
- Culprit's motivation
- Reader should be satisfied

### Chapter 28: Resolution
**Goals:**
- Justice served
- Loose ends tied
- Emotional closure
- Return to normal (changed)

---

## FAIR PLAY RULES

### Must Do
- [ ] All clues available to reader
- [ ] Culprit introduced early
- [ ] No supernatural solutions (unless established)
- [ ] Detective explains reasoning
- [ ] Solution makes logical sense

### Must Not Do
- [ ] Hidden twin/long-lost relative (without setup)
- [ ] Secret passage (without hint)
- [ ] Impossible physical feats
- [ ] Culprit without motive
- [ ] Evidence reader couldn't know about

---

## CLUE MANAGEMENT

### Types of Clues

#### Physical Evidence
- Murder weapon
- Fingerprints/DNA
- Documents
- Objects out of place

#### Testimonial
- What people say
- What they don't say
- Contradictions
- Body language

#### Circumstantial
- Opportunity
- Motive
- Behavior changes
- Financial records

### Hiding Clues
```
1. Mention in passing (easy to miss)
2. Distract with red herring
3. Character dismisses importance
4. Include with unimportant details
5. Reader knows but doesn't connect
```

---

## SUSPECT MANAGEMENT

### Each Suspect Needs
- [ ] Motive (believable)
- [ ] Opportunity (could have done it)
- [ ] Alibi (breakable)
- [ ] Secret (unrelated to crime)
- [ ] Red herring (misleading clue)

### Suspect Distribution
```
Suspect A: Obvious choice (usually innocent)
Suspect B: Has best motive (could be guilty)
Suspect C: Has best opportunity (red herring)
Suspect D: Seems innocent (often guilty)
Suspect E: Wildcard (twist option)
```

---

## TIMELINE TRACKING

### Create Timeline For
1. Day of crime (hour by hour)
2. Days leading up to crime
3. Investigation progress
4. Each suspect's movements

### Common Timeline Issues
- Character in two places at once
- Not enough time for actions
- Weather/day inconsistencies
- Events out of order

---

## RED HERRINGS

### Good Red Herrings
- Plausible but wrong
- Connect to real clues
- Have logical explanation
- Serve story purpose

### Types
1. Suspicious behavior (innocent reason)
2. False clue (planted or coincidental)
3. Wrong interpretation
4. Misleading witness
5. Partial truth

---

## DETECTIVE TYPES

### Amateur Detective
- Has personal stake
- Uses intuition
- May be in danger
- Less procedural

### Professional Detective
- Follows procedure
- Has resources
- More methodical
- Authority to investigate

### Police Procedural
- Team investigation
- Forensics focus
- Bureaucratic obstacles
- Multiple cases

---

## TIPS FOR MYSTERY

### Do's
- ✅ Plan backwards (know ending first)
- ✅ Track clues in spreadsheet
- ✅ Make detective fallible
- ✅ Give every suspect depth
- ✅ Respect reader's intelligence

### Don'ts
- ❌ Cheat the reader
- ❌ Make solution obvious
- ❌ Forget to explain clues
- ❌ Have unmotivated culprit
- ❌ Rush the reveal
